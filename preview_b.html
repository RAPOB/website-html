About

Started off as a wannabe Race Engineer in Formula SAE at Auckland, then camera specialist with Toyota GAZOO Racing. That led to the NZ Rally series — first event alongside the World Rally Championship. Formula racing is crazy, but rally is another level of insanity.

After a stint at Fisher & Paykel Healthcare and deciding medical devices wasn't it, I found autonomous robotics at Crown Equipment Corporation — working on LiDAR obstacle detection for autonomous forklifts in C++, based out of New Bremen, Ohio.

A Master's thesis followed: a year of madness in the labs, muddy ponds, creeks, and the Manukau Harbour. Two IROS papers. First class. Then a Partnership PhD with Acumino and the University of Glasgow in Biomedical Engineering. More adventures incoming.
